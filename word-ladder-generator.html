Frequently asked questions

What is a word ladder?
A word ladder (also called a doublet or word link) is a puzzle invented by Lewis Carroll in 1877. You transform one word into another by changing one letter at a time, with each step forming a valid word.
Do the start and end words need to be the same length?
Yes — in a classic word ladder both words must have the same number of letters since you can only change one letter per step without adding or removing letters.
