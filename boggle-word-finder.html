How to use the Boggle word finder

1

Enter your grid

Type each letter into the grid cells, left to right, top to bottom. Use QU for the special Boggle tile. Or hit the dice icon for a random grid.

2

Find all words

Click Find Words to see every valid word that can be traced through adjacent letters in your grid. No letter can be reused in the same word.

3

Check scores

Each word shows its Boggle point value — longer words score more. 3-letter words score 1 point, 4-letter words score 1, 5-letter words score 2, and so on.

Frequently asked questions

How does Boggle scoring work?
In standard Boggle, 3 and 4-letter words score 1 point, 5-letter words score 2, 6-letter words score 3, 7-letter words score 5, and 8+ letter words score 11 points.
What is the QU tile?
In Boggle, there is no separate Q and U tile. Instead the Q tile always shows QU and counts as two letters for word finding purposes.
Do letters have to be adjacent?
Yes — in Boggle, each letter in a word must be adjacent (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) to the previous letter. This finder checks adjacency properly.
Is it free?
