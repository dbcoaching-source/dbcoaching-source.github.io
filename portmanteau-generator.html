Frequently asked questions

What is a portmanteau?
A portmanteau is a word formed by blending the sounds and meanings of two words together. The term was popularized by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass." Famous examples include brunch (breakfast + lunch), smog (smoke + fog), and motel (motor + hotel).
What are some famous portmanteau words?
Some well-known portmanteaus include: brunch (breakfast + lunch), smog (smoke + fog), motel (motor + hotel), spork (spoon + fork), podcast (iPod + broadcast), blog (web + log), and emoji (emotion + picture in Japanese).
