Frequently asked questions

What is Mad Libs?
Mad Libs is a word game where one player asks others for words without revealing the context. The words are then inserted into a story template, usually creating a funny or nonsensical result. It was invented in 1953 by Leonard Stern and Roger Price.
Is this good for classrooms?
Absolutely — Mad Libs are a classic educational activity that teaches parts of speech (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) in a fun, memorable way. They work great as a warm-up activity or for early finishers.
What age is it for?
Mad Libs work for all ages. Younger kids love the silly results, and the game naturally teaches grammar concepts. Adults enjoy it as a party game or icebreaker activity.
Is it free?
Yes — completely free with no account or signup required. Play as many stories as you like.
